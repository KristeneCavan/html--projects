Cebu City

Cebu, known as the “Queen City of the South,” is a historically significant province in the Philippines. Originally called Sugbo, it was a thriving pre-colonial trading hub connecting merchants from China, India, and Southeast Asia. The name “Cebu” comes from “sibu” or “sibo,” meaning trade. Its history took a pivotal turn in 1521 when Ferdinand Magellan landed on its shores, introducing Christianity, now commemorated by landmarks like Magellan’s Cross and the Basilica del Santo Niño. Today, Cebu blends its rich cultural heritage with modern development, making it a vibrant destination for travelers.
